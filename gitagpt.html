WHAT I DID

Gita-GPT was designed as a mini-project for the university project in the third year of my undergrad. It is a website that is inspired by the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita. The website aims to provide a platform for users to ask questions and get answers from the Gita.

The website is a database of verses from the Gita along with their translations and explanations sorted by the specific human emotions they address. The website also has a chat feature where users can ask questions and get answers from the Gita. The chat feature is still under development and being worked on to provide most accurate answers.

Gita-GPT refers to offcial purports of the Bhagavad Gita by A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada from the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), to avoid any misinterpretation of the verses.

My role was to design the front-end of the website and develop a Python Script to scrape the verses from the Gita and store them in a MySQL database. I also worked on the sorting of the verses according to the emotions they address.

WHAT I LEARNED

Our research found out that there are many websites that provide the verses of the Gita but none of them provide the verses sorted by the emotions they address. This is a unique feature of Gita-GPT.

To build Gita-GPT, we had to deep dive into the teachings of the Gita and understand the effetcs of spirituality on human emotions. This led us to learn more and more about this concept and led us to eventually write a research paper that delved deep into the topic.

We also circulated a Google Form with a set of questions to understand the user's perspective on the importance of spirituality on mental health and therapy and collected valuable data. This information was complied into the paper 'From Traditional to Digital: Evaluating the Role of Spirituality in Mental Health and Therapy' you can read the finidngs from clicking the link below.
